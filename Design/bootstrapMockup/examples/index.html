The Enhanced Fujita Scale

The Enhanced Fujita scale (EF) is a basis of which we classify tornados strength. Below is a table that shows the differences.

  • EF0 - 65-85 mph wind gusts
  • EF1 - 86-110 mph wind gusts
  • EF2 - 111-135 mph wind gusts
  • EF3 - 136-165 mph wind gusts
  • EF4 - 166-200 mph wind gusts
  • EF5 - Over 200 mph wind gusts

Why the Enhanced Fujita scale as opposed to the original scale? well, there are a few reasons as to why...

  1. No "damage indicators"
  2. No account for construction quality or variability
  3. No definitive correlation between damage and wind speed

This new scale has been used as the new basis for categorizing tornados many times! Let's have a quick look at what each level brings in terms of damage...

EF SCALE Damage
0 Maybe a few broken branches, damaged roof shingles, bent gutters
1 Flipped cars, rolled mobile homes, snapped trees
2 Trees cut in half, garage doors pulled off, barns destroyed, can throw car
3 Houses destroyed, walls collapsed, warehouses utterly destroyed
4 Brick is destroyed, cars are flown, debris are scattered miles, buildings utterly destroyed
5 Nothing is left behind. Roads are pulled up, land is scarred.
